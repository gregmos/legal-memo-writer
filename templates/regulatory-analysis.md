# Template: regulatory-analysis

**Use when:** the question is about a specific regulation or regulator action — applicability to the company's products/services, compliance obligations, transitional provisions, enforcement risk. Default for new EU regulations (AI Act, DSA, DMA, NIS2, Data Act etc.) or significant amendments.

## Required sections (in this order)

1. **Header** — title, date, jurisdictions, query, template name.
2. **Executive Summary** — 3-5 bullets:
   - Does the regulation apply?
   - Key obligations (top 3-5).
   - Timeline / transitional provisions.
   - Top compliance gap (if any).
3. **Regulation overview** — the official identifier (e.g. Regulation (EU) 2024/1689), entry into force, key purpose. 1-2 paragraphs.
4. **Facts, assumptions and scope** — user-provided facts; assumptions from intake; scope limitations.
5. **Scope and applicability to the company** — does the regulation apply? Articulate the scope test against the company's actual activity.
6. **Obligations breakdown** — for each relevant article / chapter:
   - **Provision**: article number, short title.
   - **Substance**: what the rule requires (paraphrased, with ≤15-word direct quote if wording is operative).
   - **Application to the company**: how this maps to the company's actual practices.
   - **Compliance status / gap**: meets / partial / does not meet / cannot assess.
7. **Transitional provisions and deadlines** — phased applicability dates, grandfathering, sunset.
8. **Enforcement and risk** — competent authorities, penalty framework, recent enforcement actions (if any in case-law research).
9. **Regulator guidance** — EDPB / EC / national DPA / supervisory authority guidance (if any in doctrinal research).
10. **Recommendations** — prioritized list of actions to close gaps; include conservative / balanced / aggressive options where appropriate.
11. **Open questions** — items requiring further analysis (e.g. ambiguous scope provisions, awaited delegated acts).
12. **Sources** — numbered list.

## Tone

Formal, structured. Heading hierarchy is dense — H2 for top sections, H3 for individual articles in section 6.

## Length guidance

3000-7000 words depending on regulation breadth. Section 6 (obligations) typically the longest.

## Rules

- Always cite the official identifier of the regulation (e.g. "Regulation (EU) 2024/1689", "Directive 2002/58/EC as amended").
- Distinguish primary obligations from secondary (implementing) instruments.
- For applicability analysis, walk through the scope test explicitly — don't conclude "applies" without showing the chain.
- Regulator guidance is non-binding unless the regulation itself empowers it (mark accordingly).

## What goes in the warning banner (forced exit / manual review)

Same yellow callout for any non-approved final status.

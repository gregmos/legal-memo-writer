# Template: risk-assessment

**Use when:** the question is fundamentally "what could go wrong?" — compliance reviews, due-diligence flags, vendor risk, contract risk. Structured as risk inventory with severity and mitigation.

## Required sections (in this order)

1. **Header** — title, date, jurisdictions, query, template name.
2. **Executive Summary** — 1 short paragraph + overall risk level (Low / Medium / High / Critical).
3. **Scope, facts and assumptions** — what was assessed, user-provided facts, assumptions adopted from intake, and what was out of scope.
4. **Risk inventory** — for each identified risk:
   - **Risk N: <short name>**
   - **Description**: 1-3 sentences.
   - **Legal basis**: rule + citation.
   - **Likelihood**: Low / Medium / High (with one-line rationale).
   - **Severity**: Low / Medium / High / Critical (with one-line rationale).
   - **Inherent risk score** (qualitative or numeric if applicable).
   - **Mitigation**: actionable steps to reduce the risk.
   - **Residual risk after mitigation**: estimate.
5. **Prioritized action plan** — top risks ordered by severity × likelihood, with owners (if known) and deadlines (if applicable).
6. **Open questions / further investigation needed** — items the writer couldn't fully assess from the provided context.
7. **Sources** — numbered list.

## Tone

Analytical but accessible. Lawyers and business stakeholders both read this. Use risk vocabulary consistently (likelihood, severity, inherent, residual).

## Length guidance

1500-3500 words. Each risk in the inventory: 150-300 words.

## Rules

- ALL risks must have a legal basis (statute, regulation, case, or guidance). No vague "general concern" risks.
- Likelihood and severity must be justified, not asserted.
- Mitigation must be specific and actionable, not generic ("monitor closely" is too vague).
- If a risk cannot be reliably assessed from the research available, put it in section 6, not the main inventory.

## What goes in the warning banner (forced exit / manual review)

Same yellow callout pattern for any non-approved final status.
